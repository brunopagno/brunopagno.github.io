Woop Sicredi

* this video is in portuguese only

In my short period working for Sicredi I got the chance to actually be a part of it's digital transformation working in the Woop project. The idea was to create a completelly new digital bank and try to attract more younger clients.

I worked on the development of webservices in Java that deal with credit card operations and their data. My daily activities included developing software with Spring Boot and Netty frameworks, and Oracle database. The application I was responsible for was a data aggregation service that received and compiled all credit card information from the platform, and was responsible for publishing notifications to users.

At this time I also became more interested in Data Science since I was working with a relatively large (and interesting) amount of data. This interest caused me to take a Data Science nanodegree at Udacity which I concluded soon after leaving the company.

Master's Degree in Data Visualization

As a researcher I developed a project to monitor user's activities using several different sensor's data. The main idea was to aggregate different data sources that could provide any information related to a user and aggregate them in a single visualization. This ended up being an extensive data wrangling work to build a prototype visualization that we named "EverydayVis".

During this period I published 4 papers which can be accessed through my ResearchGate.

Looney Tunes World of Mayhem

I spent almost two years working on Looney Tunes World of Mayhem while integrating Aquiris's engineering staff.

Initially I worked developing a webtool for balancing the game. Basically it was an application that served as a operations tool allowing operators to add and remove content from the game in real time. We decided to develop this application with Grails framework. Eventually the interface's needs required us to use a dedicated frontend framework and we added a ReactJS layer for presentation.

After one year as a "tools developer" I actually got a chance to work directly with the mobile game that you see in the video above. It was then that I actually got to apply game development skills that I had only used in private projects previously.
